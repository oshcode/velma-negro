# Quick Start: Download Assets for Cat & Dog Runner Game

## Fastest Way to Get Started (5 minutes)

### Option A: Kenney.nl (Recommended - All Free CC0)

**1. Go to Kenney.nl**
Visit: https://kenney.nl/assets

**2. Download These Packs (click each link)**

Characters:
- Search "Animal Pack" - Download it
- Look for cats and dogs in the pack

Food & Items:
- Search "Food Pack" - Download it
- Includes fruits, snacks, and various food items

Obstacles:
- Search "Abstract Platformer" or "Spikes"
- Get geometric hazards

UI:
- Search "UI Pack"
- Get buttons, score displays, icons

**3. Extract and Organize**
```bash
# Extract all downloads
# Move files into your assets folders:
# - Cat/dog sprites → assets/characters/
# - Food images → assets/items/food/
# - Spikes/hazards → assets/items/obstacles/
# - UI elements → assets/ui/
```

---

### Option B: Itch.io Complete Packs (One-Stop Shop)

**1. Go to Itch.io**
Visit: https://itch.io/game-assets/free/tag-pixel-art

**2. Search for Complete Packs**
Try these searches:
- "pixel runner pack"
- "endless runner assets"
- "pixel pet platformer"
- "cute animal pixel art"

**3. Look for packs that include:**
- ✅ Animal characters (cats/dogs)
- ✅ Food/collectible items
- ✅ Obstacles (spikes, hazards)
- ✅ Background tiles
- ✅ UI elements

**4. Download and extract to your assets folder**

## Specific Asset Recommendations

### For Cats & Dogs
1. **Kenney.nl Animal Pack** - Has various cute animals
2. **OpenGameArt.org** - Search "pixel cat" and "pixel dog"
3. Create your own simple versions using Piskel.app (16x16 or 32x32)

### For Food Items
Best sources in order:
1. **Kenney.nl Food Pack** - Comprehensive collection
2. **Craftpix.net freebies** - Search "food sprites"
3. **Itch.io** - Search "pixel food pack"

Must-have food items:
- 🍎 Apple (classic!)
- 🍌 Banana
- 🐟 Fish (for cats)
- 🦴 Bone (for dogs)
- 🧀 Cheese
- 🥕 Carrot
- 🍖 Meat
- 🍪 Cookie

### For Obstacles/Hazards
1. **Kenney.nl Abstract Platformer** - Clean geometric spikes
2. Simple spikes are best (black triangles work great!)
3. You can also use:
   - 💀 Poison food (recolor good food to dark purple/green)
   - 🔥 Fire sprites
   - ⚡ Electric hazards

## After Downloading

### 1. Test Your Assets
Create a simple test:
- Load one cat sprite
- Load one food item
- Load one spike
- Make sure they all display correctly

### 2. Check Sizes
All your sprites should be similar sizes:
- 32x32 pixels OR 64x64 pixels works best
- If they're different sizes, you can resize them later

### 3. Organize Your Files
Keep it clean from the start:
```
assets/
├── characters/
│   ├── cat.png
│   └── dog.png
├── items/
│   ├── food/
│   │   ├── apple.png
│   │   ├── fish.png
│   │   └── bone.png
│   └── obstacles/
│       └── spike.png
├── backgrounds/
│   └── ground.png
└── ui/
    ├── play_button.png
    └── score_font.png
```

## Can't Find Exactly What You Need?

### Make Your Own Simple Sprites!
For an auto-runner, you don't need fancy art. Use:

**Free Pixel Art Editors:**
- **Piskel** (piskelapp.com) - Online, very easy
- **Aseprite** ($20, best for animation)
- **GIMP** (Free, powerful but complex)

**Quick Tutorial:**
1. Create 32x32 canvas
2. Draw simple cat/dog shape (use only 3-4 colors)
3. Export as PNG
4. Done!

Simple pixel art often looks better in fast-paced games anyway!
